"If you want a thing done well, do it yourself." - Napoleon Bonaparte

Napoleon Bonaparte was boen in France in 1769. One year after France conquered the island of Corsica from the Republic of Genoa. Napoleon grew up in a noble family. In 1779, his family was able to send Napoleon to the Royal Military Secondary School.

Napoleon had a hard time during his six years at Military Academy. He was a scholarship student, but he was also an outsider. He spoken French with an accent. He was skilled at math and even a prize in the subject. He also loved learning about acient history and geography. Later Napoleon studied next at the elite Military Academy in Paris. He was the first Corsican to graduate from the academy. He graduated 42nd out of a class of 58. After garduating, he became an officer an artillery regiment in 1785.
